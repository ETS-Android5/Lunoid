Definitions
Rising / falling moon
The moon is in a rising period when it is higher and higher in the sky overnight. It is descending in the opposite case. It is this aspect which is mainly used in biodynamic agriculture. The work to be done in the waning moon is as follows:
  • Transplant and plant,
  • Work the soil, bury fertilizers and soil improvers,
  • Prune to slow growth and make more vigorous,
  • Mow in the waning moon to slow the growth of the lawn,
  • Lighten,
  • Harvest the root vegetables,
  • Take the cuttings and cut,
  • Dividing and layering,
  • Collect grafts,
  • Harvest the root vegetables and bulbs.
The work to be done in the rising moon:
  • Sow,
  • Graft,
  • Harvest vegetables, flowers, fruits and leaves.
Waning / waning moon
The moon is growing when the visible part is more and more important from day to day. This aspect is not taken into account here.
Apogee
The moon is at its apogee when it is farthest from the earth, at a distance of about 406,700 km.
Perigee
The moon is at its perigee when it is closest to Earth, at a distance of approximately 356,500 km.
Node
A node corresponds to the moment when the moon intersects the plane of rotation of the Earth around the Sun. The node is either descending when the Moon is in descending or ascending when the Moon is rising.
Bad days
The apogee, perigee, the ascending or descending lunar nodes correspond to periods harmful to cultures and where it is necessary to abstain from all work, at least in the period between 5 hours before and 5 hours after the event.
Flower days
Period to be favored for work on flowering vegetables: cauliflower, artichoke, broccoli. The flower days correspond to the passage of the moon in front of a constellation in affinity with the Air element, namely Gemini, Libra or Aquarius.
Fruit days
Period to be favored for work on fruit vegetables and grains: tomato, cucumber, zucchini, beans. The fruit days correspond to the passage of the moon in front of a constellation in affinity with the Fire element, namely Aries, Leo or Sagittarius.
Root days
Period to be favored for work on root vegetables: radishes, potatoes, carrots. The root days correspond to the passage of the moon in front of a constellation in affinity with the element Earth, namely Taurus, Virgo or Capricorn.
Leaf days
Period to be favored for work on leafy vegetables: salad, sorrel, parsley. The leaf days correspond to the passage of the moon in front of a constellation in affinity with the Water element, namely Cancer, Scorpio or Pisces.
